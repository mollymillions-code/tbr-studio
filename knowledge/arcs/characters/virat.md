# Virat Kohli — Co-Owner

## Identity

- **Role:** Co-Owner of Team Blue Rising
- **Archetype:** The Icon. His image is TBR's most powerful asset.

## Two Modes of Virat Content

There are two completely different ways Virat appears in TBR content. The rules are different for each.

### Mode 1: Virat Imagery as Hook (USE FREQUENTLY, ALL FORMATS)

Virat's face, existing photos, and footage are TBR's strongest scroll-stoppers. Use them across ALL content types:

**In videos/reels:**

- **Open with Virat** (first 1-3 seconds) — Grabs attention, stops the scroll
- **Midpoint Virat** — His image sustains engagement in the middle
- **Close with Virat** — Strong finish, brand association

**In carousels:**

- Place Virat imagery between slides to boost swipe-through rate
- His image as slide 1 (hook) or woven into the middle of the carousel

**In single image posts:**

- Use Virat with the team as a poster/hero image
- His image alongside pilots, engineers, the boat

**In all formats:**

- His existing imagery can be composited into posters, graphics, and overlays
- Pair him with team shots for "full TBR" content

This uses existing imagery. It does NOT require new Virat content or his direct involvement. His photo in a carousel is not "Virat content" — it is a hook technique.

**Rules for imagery-as-hook:**

- Use in as many pieces as it fits naturally. All formats.
- Rotate through available Virat photos/footage to keep it fresh
- No limit on frequency. This is a proven growth driver.
- The imagery should connect to the content (not feel random)

### Mode 2: Dedicated Virat Content (KEEP RARE)

Direct Virat involvement: interviews, personal statements, video messages, appearances where he is the subject.

**Rules for dedicated content:**

- Maximum 2-3 dedicated pieces per season
- Save for milestone moments: season launch, first race, championship result, major announcement
- Every dedicated piece should feel like an event
- His presence in these moments should be earned by the narrative
